Mission History

Journey to the Red Planet

Every Mars rover mission begins years before launch. NASA engineers design, build, test, and refine each vehicle to survive the harsh conditions of spaceflight and the Mars surface. Below is a detailed look at how the Curiosity and Perseverance missions reached the Red Planet.

How Rovers Travel to Mars

Each rover travels inside a protective spacecraft composed of several key stages:

  • Cruise Stage: Carries the rover from Earth to Mars, complete with navigational thrusters and solar arrays.
  • Entry Vehicle: A heat shield protects the spacecraft as it enters the Martian atmosphere at thousands of miles per hour.
  • Descent Stage: For Curiosity and Perseverance, the “Sky Crane” lowers the rover gently to the surface using tether lines.
  • Lander / Rover: Once the rover touches down, the descent stage cuts the cables and flies away to crash at a safe distance.

This complex landing sequence is famously known as the “Seven Minutes of Terror.”

Curiosity Mission Timeline

  • 2004–2011: Design and construction of the Mars Science Laboratory rover.
  • November 26, 2011: Launch from Cape Canaveral aboard an Atlas V rocket.
  • August 6, 2012: Successful landing in Gale Crater using the first-ever Sky Crane system.
  • 2013: Confirmed ancient freshwater lake environments.
  • 2014–2018: Climbed Mount Sharp, analyzing sediment layers that preserve Mars’ climate history.
  • 2021–Present: Continues studying organics, methane cycles, and atmospheric chemistry.

Landing Site: Gale Crater

Chosen for its layered geology, Gale Crater holds clues to hundreds of millions of years of Martian environmental change.

Notable Technologies

  • Sky Crane Landing: Innovative descent method allowing soft touchdown.
  • Dynamic Albedo of Neutrons (DAN): Detects hydrogen below the surface.
  • Sample Analysis at Mars (SAM): An onboard chemistry lab.

Perseverance Mission Timeline

  • 2012–2019: Rover design inspired by Curiosity but upgraded for sample caching.
  • July 30, 2020: Launch from Cape Canaveral.
  • February 18, 2021: Touchdown in Jezero Crater — an ancient river delta.
  • 2021: Deployed the Ingenuity helicopter for the first powered flight on another planet.
  • 2022–2023: Collected and sealed rock core samples for future Mars Sample Return.
  • 2024–Present: Continues exploring delta regions and studying ancient sediments.

Landing Site: Jezero Crater

This region once housed a deep lake fed by a river delta. Its layered sediments make it one of the most promising locations for finding signs of ancient life.

Notable Technologies

  • Sample Caching System: Collects and stores rock cores for future return to Earth.
  • Ingenuity Helicopter: The first aircraft operated on another planet.
  • MOXIE Experiment: Demonstrates oxygen production from Mars’ atmosphere.

Future Missions

Mars Sample Return, a joint NASA–ESA initiative, aims to retrieve Perseverance’s stored samples and bring them to Earth in the 2030s. This will enable detailed laboratory analysis far beyond what rover instruments can perform on Mars.
