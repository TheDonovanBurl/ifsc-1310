Mars Rover Program Overview

The Mars Rover Program is NASA’s long-term robotic exploration initiative designed to study Mars’ climate, geology, and potential to support past life. Each rover builds on the discoveries of earlier missions.

Featured Rovers

Curiosity Rover

  • Mission Goal: Determine if Mars ever had the right conditions to support microbial life.
  • Launch: November 26, 2011
  • Landing: August 6, 2012 (Gale Crater)
  • Key Discoveries: Found ancient lakebeds, organic molecules, methane variations.
  • Technical Features: Nuclear-powered, ChemCam laser, advanced mobility system.

Perseverance Rover

  • Mission Goal: Search for signs of ancient life and collect samples for future return.
  • Launch: July 30, 2020
  • Landing: February 18, 2021 (Jezero Crater)
  • Key Discoveries: Identified ancient river delta, collected rock cores.
  • Technical Features: Sample caching system, MOXIE oxygen experiment, Ingenuity helicopter support.
